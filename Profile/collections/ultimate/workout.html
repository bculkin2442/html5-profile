Introduction to Working Out with Weights

One rule when weightlifting is to make sure to split it so that you are not over training. Doing chest on Monday, triceps on Tuesday, and shoulders on Wednesday will overtrain your triceps. Why? Because just about every chest and shoulder exercise works the triceps secondarily, and almost every back exercise works the biceps secondarily. so you would need to do one of three things.

 

1) Work chest, triceps and shoulders on the same day, and biceps and back on the same day so that is okay to work the secondary muscles, because you're doing them anyways

2) Seperate those muscles that work a secondary muscle so they are far enough apart not to overtrain you. Do chest and biceps Monday, triceps Wednesday, and shoulders and back Friday

3) Do chest and triceps Monday, shoulders Thursday, back and biceps on Friday

     While there are a variety of weightlifting routines availible, a very popular routine where you work different body parts on alternating days of the week. A sample routine is shown below

Split Routine

Monday/Thursday - Chest, Triceps and Biceps

Chest: Flat Bench Press - 4 sets, Incline Hammer - 2 sets, Dumbbell Flyes- 2 sets

Triceps: Tricep Press - 2 sets, Dips - 2 sets

Biceps: Standing Barbell Curls - 3 sets, Preacher Curls - 2 sets

 

Wednesday - Cardio

Treadmill - 20 min, Elliptical - 20 minutes

 

Tuesday/Friday - Back & Legs

Back: Barbell uprights - 3 sets, Lat Pulls - 3 sets, Seated rows - 2 sets

Legs: Squats - 4 sets, Leg Extensions - 2 sets, Leg Curl - 2 sets, Calve Raises - 3 sets

Shoulders: Dumbbell Military Presses - 4 sets, Lat Raises - 3 sets, Shrugs - 3 sets

Saturday/Sunday - Days Off

See an Ultimate Fitness trainer for advice on what routine works for you
